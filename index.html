Your GBP Optimization Action Plan

Based on your survey responses, here are prioritized action points to improve your Google Business Profile:

High Priority Actions

Claim and verify your Google Business Profile high

This is the foundation of your local online presence and unlocks all management features.

Visit business.google.com to claim your listing. You'll need to verify ownership through a postcard, phone call, or email depending on your business type.

How to do it: Search for your business on Google Maps, click "Claim this business," and follow the verification steps. If your business doesn't appear, create a new listing from the Google Business Profile website.

Complete your basic business information high

Correct contact details ensure customers can find and reach you, significantly improving local search ranking.

Ensure your business name, address, phone number, and website are accurate and consistent across all platforms. Use your exact legal business name rather than adding keywords or locations.

How to do it: Log into your GBP dashboard, go to "Info" section, and verify each field is correct. Make sure your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) exactly matches what's on your website and other directories.

Update your business hours high

Accurate hours prevent frustrated customers and negative reviews from people who arrive when you're closed.

Set your regular hours for each day of the week and add special hours for holidays or events. Remember to update if your hours change seasonally.

How to do it: In your GBP dashboard, go to "Info" → "Hours" to set regular hours. Click "Special hours" to add holiday exceptions. Consider setting a calendar reminder to review these quarterly.

Add high-quality photos high

Listings with photos receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more website clicks than those without.

Upload at least 10 high-resolution photos showing your business exterior, interior, products, and team. Ensure photos are well-lit, in focus, and show your business at its best.

How to do it: In your GBP dashboard, go to "Photos" tab and upload images in each category. Use the mobile app for instant uploads. Consider hiring a professional photographer for key shots.

Create a compelling business description high

Your description helps convince potential customers why they should choose your business.

Write a 250-750 character description that clearly explains what you do, who you serve, and what makes your business unique. Include your primary keywords naturally.

How to do it: Go to "Info" section of your dashboard, click edit next to "Business description." Focus first on your unique selling points, then include keywords potential customers might search for.

Start actively requesting reviews high

Businesses that actively request reviews get 5-10x more reviews than those who don't, significantly improving visibility.

Create a systematic process to ask happy customers for reviews. The best time is immediately after a positive interaction or completed service.

How to do it: Create a short URL for your review page (search "Google Place ID" to get yours). Send follow-up emails/texts with this link, or print cards with a QR code linking to your review page.

Medium Priority Actions

Add comprehensive product/service listings medium

Detailed listings help customers understand exactly what you offer before they contact you.

List all your main products or services with descriptions, pricing (if applicable), and photos. Group similar items into categories for better organization.

How to do it: From your dashboard, go to "Products" or "Services" tab and click "Add product/service." Include details like price range, description, and a high-quality photo for each item.

Respond to all customer reviews medium

Responding to reviews shows you value customer feedback and increases engagement by 12%.

Reply to every review—both positive and negative—within 1-2 business days. Personalize each response rather than using generic templates.

How to do it: Go to the "Reviews" tab in your dashboard. For positive reviews, thank the customer and reinforce something specific they mentioned. For negative reviews, apologize, address their concerns, and offer to make it right.

Create a negative review response protocol medium

How you handle criticism publicly can turn a negative situation into a positive impression.

Develop guidelines for addressing negative reviews professionally. Never argue or be defensive, even if the review seems unfair.

How to do it: Create a simple template with: 1) Thank them for feedback, 2) Apologize for their experience, 3) Briefly explain what happened if needed, 4) Describe how you'll fix it, 5) Invite them to discuss offline with your contact info.

Start publishing Google Posts regularly medium

Regular posts increase profile views by up to 20% and can highlight timely information.

Publish updates at least weekly with news, offers, or events. Include an image, relevant text, and a clear call to action on each post.

How to do it: From your dashboard, click "Create post" and select the type (Update, Event, Offer, or Product). Add an eye-catching image, concise text (150-300 characters), and a button linking to more information or your website.

Add videos showcasing your business medium

Videos increase engagement by 2-3x compared to static images and help showcase your business in action.

Create short (30-60 second) videos that show your location, services being performed, or customer testimonials.

How to do it: Videos don't need to be professional quality. Use a smartphone to capture authentic moments of your business in action. Upload through the "Photos" tab and select the "Video" category.

Create a Q&A section with common FAQs medium

Proactively answering questions saves customers time and reduces repetitive inquiries.

Add 5-10 frequently asked questions about your business hours, services, policies, parking, etc. Be comprehensive but concise in your answers.

How to do it: From your Business Profile on Google Search (not in the dashboard), click "Ask a question" and enter common customer questions, then answer them yourself using your business account.

Low Priority Actions

Create diverse promotional content low

Different types of posts engage different customer interests and keep your profile fresh.

Mix up your Google Posts with various content types: business updates, special offers, events, and product highlights.

How to do it: Create a simple content calendar with different post types for each week. For example: Week 1 - Product highlight, Week 2 - Special offer, Week 3 - Company news, Week 4 - Event or seasonal update.

Assign a dedicated GBP manager low

Clear ownership ensures your profile stays current and questions are answered promptly.

Designate one person to be responsible for regular monitoring and updates to your Google Business Profile.

How to do it: Add this responsibility to someone's job description. Create a recurring calendar task for weekly profile checks (15-30 minutes) to respond to questions, add posts, and check insights.

Monitor and respond to Q&A questions low

Quick responses show attentiveness and can convert interested browsers into customers.

Check for new customer questions at least weekly and provide prompt, thorough answers before others can respond with potentially incorrect information.

How to do it: Set a weekly reminder to check your profile for new questions. Consider setting up Google alerts for your business name to be notified when someone asks a question.

Enhance your business description with keywords low

Strategic keywords help your business appear in more relevant searches.

Research what terms potential customers use when searching for businesses like yours, and naturally incorporate these into your description.

How to do it: Think about what services you offer and how customers might search for them. Include those terms, your location, and any specialties. Don't force keywords—they should read naturally in your text.

Regularly update product/service information low

Current information prevents customer disappointment and complaints about outdated offerings or prices.

Set a quarterly reminder to review and refresh your product/service listings with current prices, availability, and new offerings.

How to do it: Create a calendar reminder every three months to audit all listings. Remove discontinued items, update prices, add new offerings, and refresh photos as needed.
